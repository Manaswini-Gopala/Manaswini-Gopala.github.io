Projects in Progress

Business Analytics for MSLC Operations

The Math & Science Learning Center (MSLC) provides SDSU students with additional academic support beyond lectures through one-on-one tutoring
Every student sign-in at the Center captures a piece of the student learning journey. Working with a large volume of this data in R, I uncover subject demand trends, tutor workloads, repeat visits, and peak traffic hours. These insights help streamline operations and support smarter staffing decisions.
In addition, I prepare and present dashboards as well as reports to sponsors, providing clear evidence of the Center’s impact and strengthening the case for better funding and long-term resource planning.

Supply chain Risk Analyst

Global pharma supply chains face constant risks that can disrupt operations and delay medicines. To address this, I built a risk taxonomy from 40+ manufacturers and designed a framework that translates SEC filings into actionable insights. I create dashboards to track risk frequency and severity, and I am prototyping a Python NLP script to automate risk extraction, reducing review time and strengthening visibility. This work helps leadership prioritize high-impact risks and supports more resilient, data-driven decision-making.

Sentiment Analysis for Social Media Networks

I explored political conversations on Twitter to see how people really feel about different parties. By analyzing thousands of tweets, I could spot where a party had strong support, where negativity was building, and how opinions shifted across regions. The results can offer insights that could guide political decisions.

Cross-National Supply Chain Resilience & Sustainability Analysis

I studied how the US, Japan, and Germany approach supply chain management and what makes each unique.The project highlighted each nation’s strengths and the practices they adapted from one another, painting a broader picture of how global supply chains are built to adapt and thrive.
